Normal and shear stresses are calculated on a plane at an angle theta with verticle plane as shown in figure below

sn_star=(s11+s22)/2+((s11-s22)/2)*cos(2*theta)+s12*sin(2*theta);

Sign convention - 2 (tension and clockwise shear as positive)

clear all
% s and t are vectors of normal and shear stresses for different values of theta used in for loop
s=[];t=[];
s11=300;s22=150;s12=35;
hf = figure('color','w'); ha =axes(hf,'FontName','Times New Roman','FontSize',24,'FontWeight','bold','LineWidth',2.0,'Box','on');
% ha.XLim = [-100,500]; ha.XTick = -100:100:500; ha.YLim = [-300,300]; ha.YTick = -300:100:300;
ha.Visible = 'off';
hold on
% plotting x axis
quiver(0,0,450,0,'LineWidth',2,'Color','m')
quiver(0,0,-450,0,'LineWidth',2,'Color','m')
% plotting y axis
quiver(0,0,0,300,'LineWidth',2,'Color','b')
quiver(0,0,0,-300,'LineWidth',2,'Color','b')
text(450,0,'\sigma','FontName','Times New Roman','FontSize',24,'FontWeight','bold')
text(0,300,'\tau','FontName','Times New Roman','FontSize',24,'FontWeight','bold')
i = 0;
for theta=0.0:pi/24:pi
Now use the Mohr circle convention for normal and shear stress on plane making an angle theta anticlockwise with verticle
(taking tension as positive and clockwise shear as positive)
%%
% sn_star = sn-(s11+s22)/2
i = i+1;
sn_star=(s11+s22)/2+((s11-s22)/2)*cos(2*theta)+s12*sin(2*theta);
tn=-(s12*cos(2*theta)-((s11-s22)/2)*sin(2*theta)); % since clockwise shear is positive a minus sign is used here
% plotting the plane direction at the point unde consideration
dl=160;
sn1=sn_star-dl*sin(theta); tn1=tn+dl*cos(theta);
sn2=sn_star+dl*sin(theta); tn2=tn-dl*cos(theta);
plot(sn_star,tn,'ro','MarkerSize',10.0,'markerFaceColor','r','LineWidth',2)
hold on
line([sn1,sn2],[tn1,tn2],'color','k','LineWidth',2.0)
axis 'equal'
% pause(1) % uncomment this to see how the points move as theta increases anticlockwise in the element
hold on
s=[s;sn_star];t=[t;tn];
end
plot(s,t,'k','LineWidth',3)

Conclusion

Clearly for second case all the planes passes through one point which we identify as pole...
So we can see that the pole method does not work always, it works with right sign convention which is following
1. tension positive with clockwise shear positive
2. compression positive with anticlockwise shear positive
Note: Mohr cirlce method of stress transformation (coordinate transformation) works with all second order tensors providing a way to visualize stresses on differrent plane at apoint
